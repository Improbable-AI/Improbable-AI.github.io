EyeSight: A Vision-Based Tactile Sensor for Enhanced Dexterous Manipulation

Haoshu Fang, Gabe Margolis, Pulkit Agrawal

We present EyeSight, a novel 7-DoF humanoid hand with integrated vision-based tactile sensors for enhanced whole-hand manipulation. The system features quasi-direct drive actuation for human-like strength and speed, evaluated on challenging tasks including bottle opening, plasticine cutting, and plate manipulation. Results demonstrate that tactile feedback dramatically improves task success rates, highlighting the critical role of tactile information in dexterous manipulation.

DexWrist: A Compliant Robotic Wrist for Enhanced Manipulation and Policy Learning

Zhang-Wei Hong, Nolan Fey, Pulkit Agrawal

DexWrist is a compliant robotic wrist designed to advance manipulation in constrained environments and accelerate policy learning. The system achieves human wrist-like capabilities with mechanical compliance and expanded workspace, supercharging policy learning through faster teleoperation, shorter task completion, torque transparency for simulation, and enhanced workspace for cluttered scenes.

DEXO: A Hand Exoskeleton for Teaching Robots Dexterous Manipulation In-The-Wild

Younghyo Park, Idan Shenfeld, Pulkit Agrawal

DEXO is a novel hand exoskeleton system designed to teach robots dexterous manipulation in-the-wild through natural and intuitive control. The system enables kinematic mirroring and force transparency, allowing human users to directly control a robot's dexterous hand with integrated tactile sensors. The passive exoskeleton design captures high-fidelity interaction data for learning complex tasks.

DART: Crowdsourcing Robotic Data Collection Through Cloud-Based Simulation and AR

Antonia Bronars, Aviv Netanyahu, Pulkit Agrawal

DART is a teleoperation platform designed for crowdsourcing robotic data collection through cloud-based simulation and augmented reality (AR). The system addresses scalability limitations of real-world data collection by enabling higher throughput and lower physical fatigue compared to traditional teleoperation. Policies trained on DART-collected datasets successfully transfer to reality and are robust to visual disturbances.

Residual PPO: Enhanced Robotic Control Through Residual Learning Architectures

Jyothish Pari, Richard Li, Pulkit Agrawal

We present Residual PPO, a novel reinforcement learning approach that combines Proximal Policy Optimization with residual learning architectures for enhanced robotic control. This method enables robots to learn complex manipulation skills more efficiently by building upon existing control policies and refining them through experience. Demonstrates superior performance in dexterous manipulation tasks with faster convergence.

Advanced In-Hand Object Reorientation Through Tactile Feedback and Predictive Modeling

Martin Peticco, Nitish Dashora, Pulkit Agrawal

We present advanced control algorithms for in-hand object reorientation, enabling robots to manipulate objects within their grasp without dropping them. The research focuses on developing robust control strategies that can handle complex object geometries and dynamic reorientation tasks. The system combines tactile feedback, visual perception, and predictive modeling to achieve smooth and precise in-hand manipulation.
